---

Jugaad Concept

During FFCS (Fully Flexible Credit System), students must quickly select a faculty and slot from multiple available options. In the existing VTOP interface, faculty names and slots are displayed in a tabular format that is difficult to interpret during peak registration. Similar-looking rows and poor grouping increase the chances of selecting the wrong slot or faculty.

The proposed Jugaad solution is a paper prototype that reorganizes the information by grouping faculties under each slot. Instead of displaying all records together, each slot acts as a heading and only the faculties available for that slot are listed beneath it along with seat availability. This simple UI redesign improves clarity without changing the existing registration system.


---

Description

Students often panic during FFCS because seats fill within seconds. The current interface requires students to carefully read every row to identify the correct combination of slot, faculty, and seat availability. Under pressure, students may accidentally register for the wrong slot, creating timetable clashes or preventing them from enrolling in preferred courses. A single mistake can affect the entire semester.

The redesigned interface organizes information in a more structured manner. Course details are displayed at the top, followed by slot-wise grouping of faculty members and available seats. This minimizes confusion, improves readability, and allows students to make faster and more accurate registration decisions.

Benefits

Groups faculty members according to their respective slots.

Eliminates confusion caused by repeated slot entries.

Reduces the chance of selecting the wrong faculty or slot.

Makes seat availability easier to compare.

Improves readability through a clean and organized layout.

Enables faster decision-making during FFCS.

Reduces stress and cognitive load during registration.

Can be implemented through UI changes without modifying backend functionality.

Provides a more user-friendly experience for first-time users.

Helps students avoid timetable conflicts caused by accidental selections.

Limitations

It is a paper prototype and not a functional software interface.

Backend integration is not included.

Requires usability testing with students before implementation.

Additional features such as search, filters, and timetable preview can be incorporated in future versions.



---

Improvements Over the Existing Design

Faculty names are grouped under each slot instead of repeating slot information in every row.

Course information is displayed prominently at the top.

Available seats are aligned with the corresponding faculty.

Cleaner layout with improved spacing and readability.

Reduces visual clutter and unnecessary scanning.

Minimizes registration errors caused by confusion during FFCS.

Supports quicker and more confident course selection.
